Build a simple, cute, web-based “Monthly Impact” app designed for exactly two people (David and Arlayna) to plan monthly donations and volunteering together, track what they did, and keep it emotionally meaningful and fun.

Core concept
- The app is a shared ritual: each month you press a single “Reveal” button and it selects either a donation or volunteering activity based on settings, tier weights, and your pool of causes.
- The experience feels warm, playful, and lightweight, with a cute globe mascot character (a globe with eyes, like a friendly “Father Nature”) that reacts to progress and milestones.

Users and scope
- Only for two people, not a public product.
- Two profiles exist: David and Arlayna. Each cause can be submitted by either person and keeps the “submitted by” identity.
- A simple login is fine (shared passcode or two user passcodes), but keep it minimal since this is private.

Cause and organization entries
Each entry represents a cause or organization you might donate to or volunteer at. Each entry includes:
- Name (organization or cause title)
- Type: Donation or Volunteer (some entries can be “either” if you want to allow it, but donation vs volunteer should be supported)
- Tier (three tiers):
  - Tier 1: top priority
  - Tier 2: secondary
  - Tier 3: lower priority
- “Submitted by” (David or Arlayna)
- Short description: why it matters and why you chose it (personal meaning field)
- Optional location (for local places)
- Optional link (website or info)
- Optional tags/categories (environment, mental health, animals, local community, etc.)
- Optional photo (especially useful for volunteer places, but allow for either)

Monthly selection and reveal flow
- A main “Monthly Reveal” screen with a big button that triggers the selection for the current month.
- The selection result is shown as a “reveal moment” that feels satisfying and cute.
- The algorithm uses:
  - Volunteer vs Donation frequency controlled by settings (example: volunteer 3 months/year, 6 months/year, etc.)
  - Tier weighting so Tier 1 shows up more often than Tier 2, Tier 2 more than Tier 3
  - Optional controls to tune how much more common each tier is (a simple weight per tier like 60/30/10 or similar)
- The selection pool should only pull from entries that match the chosen month’s action type (donation vs volunteer), unless “either” entries exist.
- Avoid repeating the exact same selection too frequently (light “recency” protection, like “do not repeat last month unless the pool is tiny”).
- Once revealed, the app creates a “Monthly Activity” record for that month with the chosen cause and type, and it becomes the active item to complete.

Completing the month
After the reveal, the app supports logging completion:
- If Donation:
  - Enter amount donated (number)
  - Enter date donated
  - Optional note about how it felt or what you learned
- If Volunteer:
  - Enter hours volunteered
  - Enter date volunteered
  - Optional note about how it felt or what you learned
  - Optional photo upload (a simple photo from the day)
- Mark month as completed

Tracking and dashboard
A dashboard that shows impact over time with simple visuals:
- Total money donated (lifetime)
- Total volunteer hours (lifetime)
- Breakdowns:
  - Donation totals by month and year
  - Volunteer hours by month and year
  - Breakdown by tier (Tier 1 vs Tier 2 vs Tier 3 totals)
  - Breakdown by category/tag (optional if tags exist)
- “Top causes” sections:
  - Most donated to
  - Most volunteered for
- A timeline or “Monthly Journal” list view:
  - Each month shows the chosen cause, the completion status, amount/hours, date, and the personal note
  - Thumbnails for uploaded photos where available
- A feel-good “milestones” system:
  - Examples: first volunteer month completed, 10 hours volunteered, $250 donated, $1,000 donated, 12 months of consistent action
  - The globe mascot reacts when you hit milestones (happy, proud, little celebratory animation or message)

Settings
Settings allow tuning the experience:
- Volunteer frequency per year (example options: 0, 3, 6, 9, 12 months/year)
- Tier weights (editable so Tier 1 appears more often, Tier 3 less often)
- Whether repeats are allowed within N months (basic toggle and a number)
- Optional monthly donation target and volunteer hours target (just for display and motivation)
- Optional “seasonal mode” (example: allow setting certain causes to be more likely in winter/holiday season)

UI and UX style
- The UI should feel cute, warm, and playful, but still clean and simple.
- Visual identity includes:
  - A friendly globe mascot with eyes (and small expressive reactions)
  - Soft, welcoming colors and rounded components
  - Clear “ritual” layout: a main home screen, a single big reveal button, and a simple completion form
- Make it feel like a shared relationship ritual:
  - Show “Submitted by David” or “Submitted by Arlayna” on causes
  - The reveal screen can show a brief “why this matters to us” snippet after reveal (pulling from the stored personal meaning text)
  - Keep friction low: fast navigation, minimal forms, everything mobile-friendly

Pages / screens
- Login (minimal)
- Home (this month status + big Reveal button if not revealed yet)
- Reveal result screen (cute reveal moment, cause details, and “Start this month”)
- Completion screen (enter amount/hours, date, note, photo optional)
- Causes library:
  - Add new cause
  - Edit cause
  - List/filter by tier, type, submitted by, tag
- Dashboard / Impact overview
- Monthly Journal (history of months)
- Settings

Data and persistence
- Persist everything so it works over time (causes, settings, monthly logs, totals).
- Use a straightforward approach suitable for a small private app.
- Include seed/demo data so the app looks populated immediately.

Other practical notes
- The app is intended to be used with a shared Google account in real life (not necessarily an app feature), so keep the app oriented around a shared experience and shared tracking.

  - Clean project structure
  - Mobile-friendly UI
  - All the screens above
  - Weighted monthly selection logic with volunteer vs donation frequency control and tier weighting
  - Persistent storage
  - Cute globe mascot presence throughout the UI with simple reactions to milestones and progress
